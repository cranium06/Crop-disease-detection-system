About the project

Empowering farmers with intelligent crop health.

In Nigeria, plant disease can wipe out a third of a tomato harvest before a farmer ever sees the warning signs. Expert diagnosis is rare in rural communities, and by the time symptoms are obvious, the damage is done.

CropGuard AI puts an expert eye in every pocket. Using computer vision trained on tens of thousands of leaf images, it identifies the five most damaging tomato diseases — plus healthy leaves — from a single photo, and explains what to do next in plain language. Earlier action means fewer losses, less wasted pesticide, and more food on the table.

Higher yields

Catch disease at the first lesion and protect more of every harvest.

Less pesticide

Treat the right disease at the right time — not blanket spraying.

Food security

Stronger local supply, fewer losses, more resilient rural livelihoods.

The research

Grounded in real machine learning

CropGuard AI is built on MobileNetV2 transfer learning, fine-tuned in two phases on the PlantVillage tomato dataset. It is evaluated on a held-out test set of 1,941 images it never saw during training.

The six conditions it knows

Real leaf samples from the training data — from the fast-spreading blights to the whitefly-borne virus, and a healthy leaf for comparison.

Fungal

Early Blight

Dark concentric-ring lesions with yellow haloes on lower leaves.

Fungal

Late Blight

Water-soaked lesions that spread fast under cool, moist conditions.

Fungal

Leaf Mold

Chlorotic spots above, olive-green spore masses beneath the leaf.

Viral

Yellow Leaf Curl (TYLCV)

Upward leaf curling, interveinal yellowing and severe stunting.

Bacterial

Bacterial Spot

Small angular water-soaked spots that coalesce into dark lesions.
